# HR Communications & Employee Engagement — Workday County HR Reference

## Overview

HR communications in county government reach a uniquely diverse workforce: field crews without
desk access, shift workers who never see email during business hours, multilingual staff,
and senior executives who want executive summaries. Effective HR communications drive Workday
adoption, benefits enrollment, policy compliance, and employee engagement. Poor communications
create confusion, grievances, and EEOC exposure.

## Communication Strategy Framework

### Know Your Audiences

| Audience | Preferred Channel | Access | Key Needs |
|---|---|---|---|
| Office employees | Email, intranet, Workday inbox | Constant | Detail, links, self-service |
| Field / outdoor crew | Supervisor cascade, bulletin board, mobile | Limited; no desk | Simple, visual, verbal |
| Law enforcement / fire | Roll call briefing, department email, union bulletin | Shift-based | Chain of command; union awareness |
| Part-time / seasonal | Email if available; supervisor | Irregular | Onboarding basics; benefit eligibility |
| Management / leadership | Email, direct briefing | High | Summary, action required, ROI |
| Union stewards | Formal written notice per MOU | Regular | Legal precision; partnership framing |
| Employees with limited English | Translated materials; bilingual supervisor | Varies | Plain language; visual |

## Workday as a Communication Channel

Workday has several native communication tools HR should use:

### Workday Inbox / To-Do Notifications

- Every action assigned to an employee appears in their Workday inbox
- This is the primary notification for: performance reviews, benefit elections, onboarding tasks, policy acknowledgments
- Limitation: employees who don't log into Workday won't see inbox items → combine with email

### Workday Announcements

- Navigate: Home > Announcements > Create Announcement
- Posts a banner on Workday home page
- Best for: open enrollment reminders, critical deadlines, urgent policy changes
- Limitation: only visible when logged in

### Workday Mass Email (via Business Process Notifications)

- Configure email notifications in business process routing
- Examples: "Your performance review is ready for your input" auto-sent by Workday
- HR configures these at process setup; employees receive to their work email

### Workday Surveys

- If licensed: create a survey and push to a defined audience
- Uses: exit surveys, engagement pulse surveys, open enrollment satisfaction
- Results visible to HR administrators only

## Open Enrollment Communication Campaign

Open enrollment is the highest-stakes annual HR communication. Employees who don't act may
lose coverage or miss beneficial elections.

### 5-Touch Communication Plan

| Touch | Timing | Message | Channel |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 — Save the date | 4 weeks before | Dates, what's changing, what to prepare | Email + intranet |
| 2 — Manager briefing | 3 weeks before | What managers need to know; cascade to teams | Manager meeting + email |
| 3 — Launch announcement | Day 1 | It's open! Here's how to enroll. | Workday announcement + email + bulletin boards |
| 4 — Midpoint reminder | Halfway through window | [X] days left; [Y%] of employees have not enrolled | Email to non-completers (pull from Workday) |
| 5 — Final warning | 2–3 days before close | Last chance — deadline in [X] days | Email + Workday inbox + manager cascade |

### Targeting Non-Completers in Workday

- Run Benefits > Enrollment Status Report
- Filter: enrollment status = not completed
- Export list with department and supervisor
- Send targeted reminder via Workday notification or direct manager outreach

## Policy Communication Best Practices

### Effective Policy Communication Format

**Subject line / Headline**: Action-oriented and specific
> ❌ "Important HR Update"
> ✓ "New Remote Work Policy Takes Effect March 1 — What You Need to Do"

**Lead with the "so what"**: What is changing and when?

**Who is affected**: Be specific — "all classified employees" or "employees in represented bargaining units" — don't make everyone read a policy that doesn't apply to them

**What action is required**: Acknowledge by [date] in Workday / update your schedule / speak with your supervisor

**Where to get help**: HR contact, FAQ document, office hours

**When it takes effect**: Specific date

### Policy Acknowledgment in Workday

Navigate: Worker Profile > Documents > Policy Acknowledgment

Or configure as a Workday Task:
- Assign to all employees (or specific group)
- Employee reads document (attach the policy) and checks "I have read and understand"
- Tracked by HR via Reports > Workers Requiring Action

## Employee Engagement

### County Government Engagement Challenges

| Challenge | Root Cause | HR Response |
|---|---|---|
| Low engagement scores | Bureaucratic culture; limited career mobility; pay below market | Target survey insights; career pathing; pay equity |
| High disengagement in specific departments | Leadership issue | Targeted 360 feedback; manager coaching |
| Frontline workers feel invisible | No desk access; no Workday | Mobile-first communications; skip-level meetings |
| Long-tenured employees "coasting" | No development investment; limited consequences | Performance management; learning opportunities |
| New employees leaving before 12 months | Poor onboarding; culture mismatch | Onboarding improvement; 30/60/90 check-ins |

### Employee Engagement Survey

If your county conducts an annual engagement survey:

**Frequency**: Annual (or biennial for small counties)
**Vendor options**: Gallup, Qualtrics, Culture Amp, or county-built (Google Forms for small orgs)
**Length**: 20–30 questions; response time ≤10 minutes
**Anonymity**: Must be anonymous to get honest responses; report results at department level (10+ respondents) only

**Core dimensions to measure:**
- Manager effectiveness
- Clear expectations and feedback
- Growth and development opportunities
- Recognition
- Sense of belonging and inclusion
- Work-life balance
- Connection to county mission
- Intent to stay

**After the survey — action is mandatory:**
- Share results with all employees (not just leadership)
- Identify top 2–3 county-wide themes
- Each department identifies 1–2 actions based on their results
- Track action follow-through at 6 months and next survey cycle

### Workday's Role in Engagement

- Workday Peakon (if licensed): built-in continuous engagement listening
- Workday Surveys: one-time or periodic pulse surveys
- Performance module: goal alignment supports engagement
- Learning module: development opportunities connect to engagement

## Recognition Programs

### Recognition in Workday

Workday has a built-in recognition feature (in some configurations):
- Navigate: Team > My Team > [Employee] > Send Recognition
- Types: peer-to-peer, manager-to-employee
- Visible in employee's profile recognition feed
- Does not link to compensation

### County Recognition Program Best Practices

| Program | Frequency | Award | Cost |
|---|---|---|---|
| Employee of the Month | Monthly | Plaque + $25 gift card + intranet feature | Low |
| Years of Service awards | Annual | Certificate + gift by milestone (5/10/15/20/25 yrs) | Low–Medium |
| Innovation awards | Annual | Plaque + recognition at board meeting | Low |
| Team achievement awards | Ad hoc | Public recognition + nominal gift | Low |
| Safety milestone awards | When achieved | Plaque + department celebration | Low |

> Recognition that is tied to a Workday performance rating or tied to pay must go through the
  compensation process. Recognition programs (gifts, plaques) are not compensation and don't
  need to flow through Workday.
