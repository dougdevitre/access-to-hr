# HR Director Strategic Playbook — County Government

## Overview

This playbook is for newly appointed or transitioning HR Directors in county government, or
sitting HR Directors seeking a structured framework for elevating their function. It covers
the first 90 days, annual rhythm, board/leadership relationships, strategic priorities, and
how to use Workday as a strategic tool — not just a system of record.

---

## First 90 Days as HR Director

### Days 1–30: Listen and Learn

**Stakeholder listening tour — meet with each of the following:**

| Stakeholder | What to Learn |
|---|---|
| CAO / County Executive | Their top priorities; what keeps them up at night; HR's reputation |
| Department heads (all) | Their biggest HR pain points; recent HR failures or wins |
| Finance Director | Budget process; position control; relationship between HR and Finance |
| County Counselor | Pending litigation; recent settlements; legal risk areas |
| Union stewards (each unit) | Their top grievances; trust level; what they want from HR |
| Outgoing HR Director (if accessible) | History; pending issues; sensitive files |
| HR team | Their skills; morale; workload; what they think needs to change |
| Workday/HRIS admin (IT or HR) | System health; outstanding issues; data quality |
| Payroll Director | Relationship; current issues; coordination process |

**Workday Assessment (First 30 Days)**

Pull these reports and document findings:
- Headcount by org: do the numbers make sense?
- Position vacancy report: are funded positions going unfilled?
- Turnover YTD: any departments jumping out?
- Open grievances: how many? How old?
- I-9 self-audit: are all employees covered?
- Security group audit: is access appropriate?
- Integration error log: anything broken?

### Days 31–60: Assess and Prioritize

**Produce a written assessment covering:**

1. **HR team capability**: Who is strong? Where are the skill gaps? What's the morale?
2. **Compliance risk**: What keeps you up at night? FMLA tracking? I-9? EEO?
3. **Workday health**: What is broken, outdated, or misconfigured?
4. **Labor relations**: What's the grievance climate? Any ULP charges pending?
5. **Workforce trends**: Retirement wave? High turnover departments? Hard-to-fill roles?
6. **Quick wins**: What can you fix in 30 days that builds credibility?

### Days 61–90: Build Credibility and Set Direction

**Deliver quick wins:**
- Fix the most obvious Workday configuration errors
- Clear any backlog of unprocessed job changes or pending approvals
- Schedule monthly HR Director meetings with Finance and Legal
- Launch one visible improvement (manager training, self-service enablement, etc.)

**Present your 12-month HR roadmap to the CAO:**
- Current state assessment
- Top 3 compliance risks and mitigation plan
- Top 3 strategic priorities
- Resource requirements (staff, budget, Workday modules)
- How you'll measure progress

## Annual HR Director Rhythm

### Monthly

- Review HR KPI dashboard (see hr-kpi-baseline.md)
- Meet with Finance Director (position control reconciliation; budget variance)
- Meet with County Counselor (pending litigation; labor relations)
- Review open grievances and investigation status
- Review Workday integration health with IT

### Quarterly

- Present HR metrics to CAO (turnover, vacancy, headcount, cost per hire)
- Labor relations check-in (all bargaining units)
- Workday release review (upcoming features; needed action)
- Review open investigations for timeliness
- Review expiring certifications (law enforcement, fire)

### Annually

- Workforce planning report (retirement risk, succession, gap analysis)
- Pay equity analysis
- Classification audit (job profiles, FLSA status, EEO categories)
- EEO-4 preparation and filing (survey years)
- MOU compliance audit (each bargaining unit)
- Records retention review
- Workday security group audit
- Employee engagement survey
- HR department budget development and submission
- HR team performance reviews

## Board / Commission Relations

### What the Board Needs from HR

County boards (supervisors, commissioners, council members) make decisions that directly affect HR:
- Approving new positions
- Setting pay scales and benefit packages
- Approving final budgets that fund HR
- Setting policy on sensitive matters (remote work, body cameras, compensation philosophy)

### How to Present to the Board

**Lead with numbers**: headcount, turnover rate, cost per hire, days to fill, compliance status
**Frame in mission terms**: "These vacancies in [department] affect [service delivery]"
**Quantify risk**: "Our current FMLA tracking gap creates an estimated $X in litigation exposure"
**Propose, don't just report**: "We recommend [action] because [ROI]"

## Building an HR Team

### Core HR Roles for County Government (by size)

**Small County (under 200 employees)**
- HR Director/Manager (solo or with 1 generalist)
- Key gap: no specialization; HR Director does everything
- Strategy: use Workday heavily for automation; consider shared services with neighboring county

**Mid-Size County (200–1,000 employees)**
- HR Director
- HR Generalists (1 per 150–200 employees)
- Benefits Specialist (1)
- Payroll Coordinator (may sit in Finance)
- HRIS/Workday Admin (may be IT or HR)
- Recruiter (1)

**Large County (1,000+ employees)**
- HR Director
- Deputy HR Director or Chief People Officer
- HR Business Partners (aligned to departments)
- Compensation Analyst
- Benefits Manager + Specialist
- Recruiting Manager + Recruiters
- Training and Development Coordinator
- Labor Relations Specialist
- HRIS Manager
- HR Operations/Compliance Specialist

### HR Team Skills Assessment

Evaluate your team annually on:
- Workday proficiency (self-rate + manager observation)
- Employment law knowledge (FMLA, ADA, FLSA, MHRA)
- Labor relations / union knowledge (if applicable)
- Compensation analysis skills
- Investigation skills
- Communication / relationship skills
- Data analysis and reporting skills

Gaps in skills → training, cross-training, or hiring.

## Strategic HR Priorities for County Government (2024–2027)

### 1. Retirement Wave Preparation

- 30–50% of senior county workers are retirement-eligible within 5 years in many departments
- Build succession plans; invest in internal pipeline; accelerate knowledge transfer
- Workday: succession plans for all critical positions; retirement risk report

### 2. Compensation Competitiveness

- County governments are losing talent to private sector and other government jurisdictions
- Conduct market pay study every 3 years; address compression
- Workday: pay equity analysis; compa-ratio report; merit cycle management

### 3. Workforce Diversity and Inclusion

- Community demographics shift; workforce should reflect community
- Diverse applicant pools; equitable promotion; inclusive leadership
- Workday: applicant flow report; promotion equity; EEO-4

### 4. Mental Health and Wellness

- Particularly critical for public safety; growing for all employees post-COVID
- EAP utilization; peer support programs; manager training on mental health
- Workday: track EAP referrals (not content); leave patterns

### 5. Workday Optimization

- Most counties use 30–50% of Workday's capabilities
- Progressive rollout: self-service → reporting → analytics → succession → learning
- Workday: track self-service adoption; schedule implementation-optimization.md audit

### 6. Manager Development

- "Employees don't leave organizations; they leave managers"
- New supervisor training; 360 feedback; coaching resources
- Workday: new supervisor learning path; performance cycle quality metrics

## HR Director Self-Assessment

Use annually to identify personal development areas:

| Competency | Questions to Ask Yourself |
|---|---|
| Legal knowledge | Am I current on FMLA, ADA, FLSA, MHRA changes? |
| Labor relations | Do I understand our MOUs deeply? Am I trusted by union leadership? |
| Workday proficiency | Can I run any report our team needs? Do I understand our config? |
| Financial acumen | Can I defend our HR budget? Can I cost a union proposal? |
| Strategic thinking | Am I solving today's problems or also planning for 3 years out? |
| Leadership | Is my team growing? Is morale strong? Am I developing my successor? |
| Board relationships | Do board members trust my judgment? Do I know their priorities? |
| Communication | Are my reports clear? Do employees trust HR? |
